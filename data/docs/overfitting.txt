Overfitting occurs when a model learns the training data too well, including noise and outliers.

This results in high training accuracy but poor performance on unseen data.

Common causes:
- Too many features
- Small dataset
- Highly complex models

Prevention techniques:
- Cross-validation
- Regularization (L1, L2)
- Dropout (in neural networks)
- Early stopping
- Increasing dataset size

Underfitting is the opposite problem, where the model fails to capture patterns in the data.
